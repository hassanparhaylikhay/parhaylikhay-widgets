Cone, Sphere & Cylinder — Volumes
All three have the same radius r, and the cylinder/cone height = 2r. The ratio of their volumes is always 1 : 2 : 3 — Archimedes was so proud of this he asked for it on his tombstone.

Cambridge tip: Vcone = \(\frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h\) — the \(\frac{1}{3}\) is crucial, examiners see it omitted constantly. For a sphere: V = \(\frac{4}{3}\pi r^3\). Both formulas are printed on the exam paper, but you still lose marks if you substitute wrong values.
