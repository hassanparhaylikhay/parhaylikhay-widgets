Cylinder — Unrolling the Curved Surface
Change the radius — watch how the rectangle width on the right changes. The curved surface of a cylinder is just a rectangle in disguise: width = 2πr, height = h.

Cambridge tip: for total surface area, don't forget both circular ends. Examiners see students write 2πrh and stop — that's only the curved SA. The full answer is \(2\pi r h + 2\pi r^2\). If the question says "open cylinder" (no lid/base), use just \(2\pi r h\).
