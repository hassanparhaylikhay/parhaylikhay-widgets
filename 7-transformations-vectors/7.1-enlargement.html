Enlargement — Scale Factor & Rays from Centre
Drag the yellow dot to choose the centre of enlargement. Slide k through zero and negative — the image jumps to the other side of the centre. Rays always pass through the centre and through both object and image.

Cambridge tip: a negative scale factor means the image is on the opposite side of the centre and is also rotated 180°. The mark scheme always wants: "enlargement, scale factor k, centre (x, y)." For fractional k (e.g. ½): the image is smaller — this is still called an enlargement.
