Histograms — Why Frequency Density?
These classes have unequal widths. Toggle between "frequency" and "frequency density" as the y-axis. Watch which bar looks tallest — and why that's misleading when class widths differ. Area = frequency. Always.
Cambridge tip: frequency density = frequency ÷ class width. In a histogram, it's the area of each bar (not the height) that equals the frequency. For equal class widths the distinction doesn't matter — but Cambridge always uses unequal widths to test this.
