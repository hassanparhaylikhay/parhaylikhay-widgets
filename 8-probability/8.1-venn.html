Cambridge tip: the addition rule P(A∪B) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A∩B) is always needed when events can both happen. If events are mutually exclusive, P(A∩B) = 0, so P(A∪B) = P(A) + P(B). If P(A) + P(B) > 1, they cannot be mutually exclusive.
