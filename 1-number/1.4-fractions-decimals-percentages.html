Cambridge tip: a terminating decimal has a denominator with only factors 2 and 5. Recurring decimals are still rational — write them with dot notation (0.3̄ for 1/3). To convert a fraction to a percentage, divide then multiply by 100 — don't forget the % sign or you lose the mark.
