Key rule: A 15% increase gives multiplier 1.15. To reverse: original = new ÷ 1.15.
Never subtract 15% of the new value — that gives the wrong answer.
Cambridge tip: compound interest formula A = P(1 + r/100)n — write it with the multiplier in one step. Simple interest earns the same amount every year; compound interest grows faster because interest is earned on interest. The difference is worth a separate mark.
