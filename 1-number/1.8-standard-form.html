Standard Form

A × 10n  where  1 ≤ A < 10

Quick examples
× 10
Cambridge tip: A must satisfy 1 ≤ A < 10 — if A = 10 or more, you lose the mark. Positive n for numbers ≥ 10 (large), negative n for numbers between 0 and 1 (small). For calculations in standard form, handle the powers of 10 separately and adjust A back into range at the end.
