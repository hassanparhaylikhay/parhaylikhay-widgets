Cambridge tip: to rationalise 1/√n, multiply top and bottom by √n — the denominator becomes the whole number n. For a conjugate denominator (a + b√c), multiply by (a − b√c): the denominator becomes a² − b²c. You can only add/subtract surds if the radicands match — simplify both first, then combine like terms.
